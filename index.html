Frequently Asked

Próspera is a private charter city on the island of Roatán, Honduras. Founded in 2017 under Honduras's ZEDE law (Zona de Empleo y Desarrollo Económico), it operates as a special jurisdiction with its own legal code, tax structure, and governance independent of Honduran national administration. It is developed by Honduras Próspera Inc. and designed as a platform for business formation, residency, and innovation in sectors like biotech, crypto, and medical services.

Próspera offers three tiers of access. A Visitor Pass (free) allows unlimited daily access and 30 overnight stays per year. E-Residency ($130/year) provides a digital identity and business formation rights for remote entrepreneurs. Full Residency ($130/year plus mandatory insurance) grants full-time access and all business rights.

Apply at eprospera.hn. The process requires identity verification, a background check, and signing the Agreement of Coexistence. Processing typically takes one to three days.

The corporate income tax rate is approximately 10%, compared to Honduras's standard 25%. There is no capital gains tax, no wealth tax, and no personal income tax on income sourced outside Próspera. Business formation can be completed online in under 24 hours.

The legal system is common law-based and allows parties to select courts in the Cayman Islands or Delaware as dispute resolution forums. The regulatory framework is designed to be permissive for emerging industries, including longevity medicine, biotech, and digital assets.

Over 250 companies have incorporated in Próspera as of 2025. Active sectors include crypto and fintech, longevity and biotech research, medical tourism, real estate development, and financial services. Coinbase invested in 2025. Minicircle operates a gene therapy clinic on site. The campus includes Próspera Residences, a hotel and co-working complex, and a medical center under development.

Honduras repealed the ZEDE law in April 2022 and the Supreme Court declared the zones unconstitutional in September 2024. Próspera disputes retroactive application, citing a 50-year contractual stability agreement, and continues to operate on Roatán. The company filed an ICSID arbitration case under CAFTA-DR in late 2022, which survived preliminary objections in February 2025 and is now at the merits phase. The political environment shifted in early 2026 when President Asfura, who is more favorable toward the zones, took office.
