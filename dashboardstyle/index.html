FAQ

Próspera is a private charter city on the island of Roatán, Honduras. Founded in 2017 under Honduras's ZEDE law, it operates as a special jurisdiction with its own legal code, tax structure, and governance independent of Honduran national administration. It is developed by Honduras Próspera Inc. and designed as a platform for business formation, residency, and innovation in sectors like biotech, crypto, and medical services.

Próspera offers three tiers. A Visitor Pass (free) allows unlimited daily access and 30 overnight stays per year. e-Residency ($130/year) provides a digital identity and business formation rights. Full Residency ($130/year plus mandatory insurance) grants full-time access and all business rights. Apply at eprospera.hn. Processing typically takes one to three days.

The corporate income tax is approximately 10%, versus Honduras's standard 25%. There is no capital gains tax, no wealth tax, and no personal income tax on income sourced outside Próspera. Business formation can be completed online in under 24 hours. The legal system is common law-based; parties may select courts in the Cayman Islands or Delaware for dispute resolution.

Over 250 companies have incorporated in Próspera as of 2025. Active sectors include crypto and fintech, longevity and biotech research, medical tourism, real estate development, and financial services. Coinbase invested in 2025. Minicircle operates a gene therapy clinic on site. The campus includes Próspera Residences, a hotel and co-working complex, and a medical center under development.

Honduras repealed the ZEDE law in April 2022 and the Supreme Court declared the zones unconstitutional in September 2024. Próspera disputes retroactive application, citing a 50-year contractual stability agreement, and continues to operate on Roatán. The ICSID arbitration case filed under CAFTA-DR survived preliminary objections in February 2025 and is now at the merits phase. A more business-friendly administration took office in January 2026.
